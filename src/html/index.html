Decline in household income

From 2007 to 2015, only 7 of the 39 Florida counties with reliable data experienced an increase in household income. During that period, Florida's median household income — adjusted for inflation — dropped 7.2 percent (from $ 53,272 to $49,426), and nationally it dropped 2.4 percent (from $ 57,165 to $55,775).

Sources: Quarterly Census of Employment and Wages, Bureau of Labor Statistics; Florida International University Metropolitan Center
